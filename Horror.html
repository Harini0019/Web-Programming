Beginner's Guide to Horror

Extreme to Light

Incantation

Sleep with your lights on because this movie is that scary..
Incantation is a spine-tingling supernatural horror film that will leave you questioning reality long after the credits roll. This Taiwanese horror film takes you on a chilling journey through the world of curses, dark rituals, and the supernatural. The story follows a mother who, after breaking a taboo and unleashing a curse, must protect her daughter from the malevolent forces that now haunt them. What sets Incantation apart is its unsettling atmosphere, mixed with a complex narrative that blends found footage with traditional storytelling.
The horror is not just in the eerie visuals or jump scares, but in the psychological terror that builds throughout the film. The sense of dread is palpable, and the twists and turns keep you guessing until the very end. It’s a film that lingers, leaving you with an overwhelming sense of unease. If you're a fan of dark, atmospheric horror that plays with perception and reality, Incantation is a must-watch.

Rating: 10/10

Haunting of Hill House

Every family has its secrets. Their's were just sinister and twisted...
The Haunting of Hill House is not just another ghost story—it’s a beautifully tragic exploration of family, trauma, and the supernatural. Directed by Mike Flanagan, this Netflix series takes you on a spine-chilling ride through the haunted halls of Hill House, where every corner holds a secret, and every shadow whispers of past horrors. What makes this show stand out isn’t just the terrifying spirits or the eerie atmosphere, but its deep emotional core.
The show masterfully intertwines the past and present, revealing how the ghosts of Hill House haunt not just the house, but the very souls of the family members. The acting is exceptional, especially from the adult cast, who bring raw emotion to their roles. This isn’t just a ghost story; it’s a poignant tale of loss, love, and the ghosts that live inside us.

Rating: 9.8/10

I saw the Devil

Asian make the most peak horrors and this movie is the proof.
I Saw the Devil is a dark, brutal, and unrelenting thriller that will leave you on the edge of your seat. Directed by Kim Jee-woon, this South Korean film takes the audience on a harrowing journey of revenge, where morality is tested and the line between good and evil blurs. The story follows a special agent who embarks on a vengeful pursuit of a sadistic serial killer who murdered his fiancée. What ensues is a chilling cat-and-mouse game that explores the psychological toll of vengeance and the limits of human endurance.
The film is violent and graphic, but it’s the emotional and psychological depth that elevates it from just a revenge flick. The performances are extraordinary, with Lee Byung-hun delivering a heartbreaking portrayal of a man consumed by grief and rage, while Choi Min-sik’s portrayal of the villain is both terrifying and mesmerizing.

Rating: 9.2/10

The Taking of Deborah Logan

Imagine a documentary crew following an elderly woman’s battle with dementia, only to uncover something far darker. The Taking of Deborah Logan takes the found-footage genre to terrifying new heights, blending the heartbreaking reality of Alzheimer’s with a supernatural horror that’s impossible to shake. As the camera rolls, you watch Deborah, once a vibrant woman, slowly descend into madness—but what’s truly horrifying is that her madness might not be a result of her illness at all. The slow-burn tension builds to a gut-wrenching climax, and Jill Larson’s portrayal of Deborah is nothing short of terrifying. It’s a film that taps into real-world fears, making the supernatural even more unsettling. It’s a twisted, psychological ride that will have you questioning what’s real—and what’s lurking in the shadows.

Rating: 9.3/10

Supernatural

Saving people, hunting things. The family business.
Supernatural is a wild, genre-defying ride that combines family drama, monster hunting, and epic battles between good and evil. The show follows brothers Sam and Dean Winchester as they traverse the American heartland, battling everything from vengeful spirits to ancient gods. What starts as a simple monster-of-the-week format quickly evolves into an intricate, high-stakes story about fate, destiny, and the endless war between heaven and hell.
The chemistry between the leads, Jared Padalecki and Jensen Ackles, is what really makes the show shine. Their dynamic is both heartwarming and hilarious, giving the series a perfect balance of humor and emotion amidst the chaos. With its mix of urban legends, supernatural lore, and jaw-dropping plot twists, Supernatural keeps you hooked for all 15 seasons. Whether you're in it for the thrilling hunts, the complex mythology, or the heart-wrenching moments, this series delivers it all with a side of witty banter and unforgettable characters. It's a supernatural adventure that you won’t want to end.

Rating: 9.0/10

Stranger Things

Stranger Things is a nostalgic, supernatural rollercoaster that will make you want to grab your walkie-talkie and rally your friends for an adventure. Set in the 1980s, this series is a love letter to everything we adore about that era—sci-fi, adventure, and of course, a healthy dose of terror. When a young boy goes missing in the small town of Hawkins, Indiana, his friends uncover a secret world of government experiments, otherworldly creatures, and a girl with strange powers.
It’s The Goonies meets E.T. meets The X-Files, with a dash of horror thrown in for good measure. The chemistry between the kids is heartwarming, the mysteries are compelling, and the suspense is unrelenting. Stranger Things is an emotional rollercoaster with a perfect blend of thrills, heart, and 80s nostalgia that keeps you coming back for more, season after season.

Rating: 9.1/10
